# Motorcycle Maintenance Knowledge Base for RAG (Fred AI)

## Introduction

This document contains comprehensive motorcycle maintenance knowledge
designed for use in a Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) system. It
covers routine service, troubleshooting, riding care, safety, cost
management, and best practices.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Core Maintenance Principles

-   Regular maintenance prevents breakdowns
-   Small issues become expensive if ignored
-   Clean bikes last longer
-   Fluids are the lifeblood of motorcycles
-   Tires affect safety more than any other component

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Oil & Fluids

### Engine Oil

-   Change every 3,000--5,000 km (or manufacturer spec)
-   Always replace oil filter
-   Warm engine before draining
-   Use correct viscosity

### Brake Fluid

-   Replace every 1--2 years
-   Keep reservoir clean
-   Never mix fluid types

### Coolant (Liquid cooled bikes)

-   Replace every 2 years
-   Use motorcycle-specific coolant

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Chain & Drivetrain

### Chain Maintenance

-   Clean every 500--1,000 km
-   Lube after cleaning
-   Adjust slack properly
-   Inspect for rust & stiff links

### Sprockets

-   Replace when teeth are hooked
-   Usually changed with chain

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Tires

-   Check pressure weekly
-   Inspect for cracks or punctures
-   Replace when tread is worn
-   Old rubber loses grip

Recommended pressure: - Follow manufacturer sticker/manual

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Brakes

-   Inspect pads regularly
-   Replace before metal contact
-   Check disc thickness
-   Watch for vibration or squealing

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Battery

-   Keep terminals clean
-   Charge if bike unused
-   Replace every 2--4 years
-   Weak batteries cause hard starts

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Air Filter

-   Clean every 5,000--10,000 km
-   Replace if damaged
-   Dirty filters reduce performance

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Spark Plugs

-   Inspect for deposits
-   Replace at service interval
-   Misfires indicate plug issues

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Suspension

-   Check fork seals for leaks
-   Replace fork oil periodically
-   Inspect rear shock

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Cables & Controls

-   Lube throttle & clutch cables
-   Ensure smooth movement
-   Adjust clutch free play

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Common Problems & Causes

### Hard Starting

-   Weak battery
-   Dirty spark plugs
-   Fuel delivery issues

### Poor Acceleration

-   Dirty air filter
-   Worn chain
-   Fuel issues

### Overheating

-   Low coolant
-   Blocked radiator
-   Lean mixture

### Brake Fade

-   Old fluid
-   Worn pads
-   Overheating

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Routine Maintenance Schedule (Typical)

Every Ride: - Check tires - Inspect brakes - Check lights

Weekly: - Chain lube - Tire pressure

Monthly: - Oil level - Bolt inspection

Every 3--6 months: - Oil change - Air filter check

Yearly: - Brake fluid - Coolant - Full inspection

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Cleaning Tips

-   Use mild soap
-   Avoid pressure washer on bearings
-   Dry completely
-   Lube chain after wash

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Riding Habits That Extend Bike Life

-   Warm engine before hard riding
-   Avoid over-revving
-   Shift smoothly
-   Keep RPM in optimal range

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Cost Saving Tips

-   Do basic maintenance yourself
-   Buy parts in kits
-   Track expenses
-   Prevent damage early

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Safety Essentials

-   Good tires save lives
-   Brakes must be perfect
-   Lights must work
-   Helmet and gear always

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Storage Tips

-   Use fuel stabilizer
-   Disconnect battery
-   Inflate tires properly
-   Cover bike

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Seasonal Maintenance

Rainy season: - Lube chain often - Check brakes

Hot season: - Monitor coolant - Check tire pressure

Cold storage: - Battery care - Fuel stabilizer

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Maintenance Mindset

A motorcycle is a machine that rewards care.\
Routine attention prevents breakdowns and saves money.
